Wildlife Crime Guidelines

Helpline Numbers

  • Wildlife Crime Control Bureau (WCCB): 1800-11-0404
  • Forest Department Helpline: 1926
  • Local Forest Officer: +91-XXXXXXXXXX

What to Do

  • Take photos/videos only if it is safe.
  • Note location, time, and details.
  • Call the nearest forest officer or helpline immediately.
  • Stay calm and maintain safe distance from animals.

What NOT to Do

  • Do NOT touch or go near wild animals.
  • Do NOT confront poachers.
  • Do NOT share sensitive wildlife locations online.
  • Do NOT panic or run.

Illegal Wildlife Trade

Illegal wildlife trade includes buying or selling protected animals, their skins, bones, feathers, or any products made from them. It is a serious crime that threatens biodiversity and endangers species. If you witness suspicious activity, report it immediately.

Based on India's Wild Life (Protection) Act, 1972, and other related laws, here is a short list of common wildlife crimes:

  • Illegal hunting and poaching: Killing, capturing, or poisoning wild animals.
  • Illegal possession and trade: Unlawfully owning or trading in wild animals, their meat, or their parts, such as skins, bones, and horns.
  • Habitat destruction: Damaging or encroaching on the habitat of wild animals, particularly within national parks and sanctuaries.
  • Illegal plant trade: The unauthorized collection, sale, or possession of specified plant species.
  • Wildlife cybercrime: Illegally trading protected species and their parts online through social media and other platforms.
  • Animal cruelty: Molesting, injuring, or otherwise causing harm to any wild animal.
